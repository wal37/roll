Services

Implementation support for clinical adoption.

RadicAI services guide healthcare organizations from early workflow discovery through launch and optimization, making sure AI-assisted reporting fits clinical operations, governance requirements, and day-to-day radiologist behavior.

01

Workflow Assessment

We review how studies move from image acquisition to final report, including dictation habits, turnaround pressure, quality review, critical result handling, and prior-study comparison needs.

02

Deployment Planning

We help define rollout scope, user roles, security expectations, integration touchpoints, approval steps, and operational responsibilities before the platform is introduced to readers.

03

Template Optimization

We refine report structures, common phrases, impression patterns, and specialty-specific language so generated drafts match the organization's reporting style and clinical standards.

04

Training & Enablement

We provide onboarding support for radiologists, administrators, and quality teams, focusing on when to use AI assistance, how to review outputs, and how to provide useful feedback.

05

Quality Review

We evaluate draft quality, terminology consistency, finding-to-impression alignment, and user feedback after launch to identify areas that need tuning or workflow adjustment.

06

Ongoing Support

We support workflow updates, new reporting preferences, service-line changes, and team questions as the organization expands or adjusts AI-assisted reporting over time.

Security

Built for protected clinical environments.

The platform message centers on privacy, deployment control, and enterprise readiness for healthcare organizations. RadicAI is presented for environments where clinical data must be handled carefully, access needs to be controlled, and every AI-assisted output should remain reviewable by qualified professionals.

Security planning supports responsible adoption from the first evaluation through production use, including governance discussions, user permissions, audit expectations, and clear boundaries around clinical responsibility.

Protected data handling

Designed around clinical data governance, access controls, and traceable interactions. Sensitive information should be handled only through approved workflows, with clear expectations for storage, processing, and authorized use.

  • Role-based access planning
  • Secure handling of clinical content
  • Governance-aligned deployment review

Operational fit

Works with radiology workflows instead of forcing teams into a separate authoring process. Security controls are considered alongside usability so radiologists can work efficiently without bypassing required safeguards.

  • Workflow-aware user permissions
  • Administrative oversight support
  • Deployment planning for enterprise teams

Reviewable outputs

Keeps the radiologist as final authority with clear drafts, edits, and validation points. AI-generated content is positioned as assistance, not a replacement for clinical judgment or final report approval.

  • Human review before sign-off
  • Visible draft and edit process
  • Quality review and accountability support
